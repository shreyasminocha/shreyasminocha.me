It's **Shreyas Minocha**. That's pronounced [shr-AY-us min-o-cha](/name.flac) (_cha_ as in _chai_ or _ch_ as in _channel_). In IPA, I believe that's [/ʃrˈeɪəs ˌmɪnoˈtʃä/](https://ipa-reader.com/?text=%CA%83r%CB%88e%C9%AA%C9%99s%20%CB%8Cm%C9%AAno%CB%88t%CA%83%C3%A4&voice=Aditi).

In Devanagari, it's <span lang="hi">श्रेयस मिनोचा</span> and in Nastaliq, I believe it's <span lang="ur">شْرییَسَ مِنوچَا</span>. If you can read those, you probably already knew how to pronounce the name.

I also have the Chinese names <ruby lang="zh-CN">学<rp>(</rp><rt>xué</rt><rp>)</rp>呀<rp>(</rp><rt>ya</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby> and <ruby lang="zh-CN">雪<rp>(</rp><rt>xuě</rt><rp>)</rp>鸭<rp>(</rp><rt>yā</rt><rp>)</rp>子<rp>(</rp><rt>zǐ</rt><rp>)</rp></ruby>.
